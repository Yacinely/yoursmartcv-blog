## Why Use Power Words in Your CV?

Power words for CV crafting are descriptive action verbs and phrases that show your accomplishments clearly and confidently. They replace vague statements like "responsible for" with more dynamic terms such as "led," "managed," or "developed."

These words help demonstrate impact. For example, saying "implemented a new filing system that improved efficiency" is stronger and more specific than "helped organize documents." Recruiters and hiring managers are naturally drawn to CVs that use such compelling resume action verbs.

Power words also help your CV stand out when recruiters skim through many applications. They add a professional tone and make your skills and experience easier to quantify.

## How Power Words Help with ATS Screening

Applicant Tracking Systems scan your CVs for relevant job application keywords to decide whether to move your resume forward. These systems look for terms specifically related to the job description.

Using the right ATS-friendly power words improves your chance of matching these keyword requirements. Without them, your CV might get overlooked before it even reaches a human reviewer.

According to TopCV, 28% of resumes are rejected by ATS due to missing keywords. This highlights the importance of using the correct vocabulary, including powerful action verbs, to ensure your CV is considered.

It’s important to note that different ATS software may vary in keyword sensitivity and parsing rules, so careful CV tailoring for the specific job is vital.

## Choosing the Best Power Words to Use in a CV

Identifying which power words to use depends on your industry, role, and the specific job description. Here are some general guidelines:

- Align with the job requirements. Scan the job posting and pick out keywords and phrases the employer emphasizes.
- Use a CV power words list or thesaurus of strong action words for CV that relate to your tasks and achievements.
- Mix in various verbs to avoid repetition and show versatility.
- Match the tone and level of responsibility expected in the job. Senior roles typically require more authoritative words like "directed" or "orchestrated."

## Tips for ATS Optimization and CV Tailoring

To ensure your CV stands out to both ATS and recruiters, keep these points in mind:

- Customize your CV keywords for each job application. Generic resumes tend to perform worse in ATS screening.
- Avoid stuffing your CV with keywords unnaturally; instead, embed them logically in your work experience and achievements.
- Use standard job titles and clear headings to help ATS understand your CV structure.
- Stick to common fonts and simple formatting to help ATS parse your content correctly.
- Proofread your CV to ensure clarity and that your chosen power words fit the context well.
