## What Are CV Keywords for ATS?

CV keywords for ATS are terms or phrases that applicant tracking systems look for when scanning CVs. These generally include skills, qualifications, job titles, technologies, certifications, and action verbs that appear in the job description or are standard in your industry.

They differ slightly from traditional resume keywords because ATS requires more exact matching to parse and rank CVs appropriately. For example, if a job listing mentions "project management," including that exact phrase in your CV is necessary for ATS optimization, rather than a generic mention of managing projects.

The goal is CV tailoring with keywords so your CV fits the job description languages while remaining truthful and clear.

## Why Using the Right Keywords Matters

Applicant tracking systems are programmed to scan CVs before any recruiter even sees them. According to Inteller.ai, over 97% of Fortune 500 companies use an ATS to filter resumes before human review. In fact, approximately 77% of resumes are eliminated by ATS before they reach a hiring manager.

This filtering means that if your CV lacks the right applicant tracking system keywords, it may never be considered, even if you have the right experience. Using the best keywords for CVs is not about stuffing your CV but matching the job description keywords naturally.

This emphasizes the importance of CV keyword optimization to improve your chances of reaching the interview stage.

## How to Find Keywords for My CV to Pass ATS

To know what keywords should I use in an ATS-friendly CV, start by carefully reading the job description. Look for repeated skills, qualifications, and responsibilities mentioned throughout. These are your primary keywords.

Other methods include:

- Checking industry-specific job postings to identify common resume keywords.
- Using tools like keyword analyzers or CV optimization resources to highlight effective keywords.
- Looking at company websites or LinkedIn profiles in your target role to spot jargon and skills frequently referenced.

When you combine these approaches, you develop a solid list of CV keywords for ATS that reflect the role and industry accurately.

## Where and How to Use ATS CV Keywords in Your CV

Once you have your list, you’ll want to use keywords thoughtfully across your CV:

- **Professional Summary**: Integrate high-impact keywords describing your experience and skills.
- **Skills Section**: List technical and soft skills relevant to the job description keywords.
- **Work Experience**: Use keywords when describing responsibilities and achievements but ensure it reads naturally.
- **Certifications and Education**: Include keywords related to qualifications required for the role.

Avoid keyword stuffing, which can make the CV hard to read and might reduce its chances in an applicant tracking system. Keywords should support your CV content to create a balanced, clear presentation.

## Common Mistakes with CV Keyword Optimization

Common pitfalls to watch out for include:

- Using irrelevant or generic keywords unrelated to the specific job.
- Copy-pasting whole blocks of keywords without context.
- Ignoring variations of keywords (e.g., "digital marketing" vs. "online marketing").
- Overlooking keywords in key CV sections like the summary.
- Forgetting to update keywords for different job applications.

Being mindful of these issues will keep your CV clear, relevant, and aligned with what ATS looks for.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What exactly are ATS keywords?**  
ATS keywords are specific terms and phrases that applicant tracking systems look for in CVs, usually taken from job descriptions and industry jargon.  

**2. How do I find keywords for my CV to pass ATS?**  
Start by reviewing the job description for repeated terms, required skills, and qualifications. Look at similar job listings and professional profiles for additional keywords.  

**3. Can I just copy the job description keywords into my CV?**  
It’s better to use keywords naturally within your CV. Copying and pasting whole phrases may come across as forced and reduce readability.  

**4. Do I need to change keywords for every job application?**  
Yes. Tailoring your CV keywords to each specific job description improves your ATS optimization and relevance to the role.  

**5. Will using ATS CV keywords guarantee my CV gets selected?**  
No. Keywords improve the chance of passing ATS filters, but you still need clear and compelling content for recruiters to consider.  

**6. How important is the skills section for keywords?**  
Very important. A concise and relevant skills section can help highlight key applicant tracking system keywords and support your CV’s focus.  

**7. Can ATS handle synonyms or related terms?**  
Some systems recognize variations, but it’s safer to include the exact job description keywords to ensure your CV is properly parsed.
