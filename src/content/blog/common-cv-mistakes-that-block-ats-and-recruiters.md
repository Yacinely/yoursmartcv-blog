Many candidates have strong skills and good experience but still get no interviews. Very often, the problem is not their background. It is their CV.

Here are some common mistakes that can hurt your application.

## 1. A layout that is too complex

Very colorful templates, too many columns, icons, and shapes can make your CV harder to read.

Keep it simple:
- one clear column
- readable headings
- normal text

## 2. Important information hidden in graphics

If job titles, dates, or skills are inside shapes or images, ATS tools may not read them properly.

Make sure important information is written as normal text.

## 3. No clear keywords

Recruiters and ATS systems search for words that match the role.

Use keywords from the job description naturally in:
- your summary
- your skills
- your experience bullets

## 4. Too many duties, not enough results

A CV should not only say what you did. It should also show what you achieved.

Try adding results like:
- increased conversion by 10%
- reduced churn by 8%
- improved reporting speed

## 5. A CV that is too generic

If the same CV is sent to every job, it often feels too broad.

Tailor your CV to the role you want.
